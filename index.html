COVID-19 ALERT

To help reduce likelihood of spread and exposure at the Airport Animal Emergi-Center, we have taken a series of precautionary steps in response to the Covid-19 outbreak, including increased cleaning and sanitizing procedures for our clinic and limiting client interaction where appropriate.

We are limiting the number of people coming in and out of the clinic to reduce transmission and keep you and our animal hospital family safe.

Curbside service: We ask that you stay in your car and phone us (317-248-0832) and we will come out to you to have you fill out the required paperwork, and we will bring your pet into the hospital. The doctor will examine your pet and then phone you with findings, recommendations, and treatment options. After treatments have been administered we will bring your pet out to you along with all needed medication and instructions.

If you or a family member with whom you reside are under voluntary or mandated quarantine for Covid-19 or if you are waiting for testing results, then you must remain in your car while we care for your pet.

Our cleaning process includes thoroughly cleaning with a virucidal solution all items after each client/pet interaction:

  • Tables and counter-tops
  • Door handles and doorknobs
  • Workstations and keyboards in exam room and treatment areas
  • Stethoscopes
  • Pens and clipboards
Also, several times each hour, we clean any area of potential pet/client interaction including but not limited to the vestibule, lobby, waiting rooms and reception. We have also placed hand sanitizers in multiple locations throughout the clinic to encourage hand hygiene.

Emergency veterinary care you can trust!

When your dog or cat has a medical emergency and your veterinarian is not available, an after hours emergency clinic is your best choice for help. The Airport Animal Emergi-Center was established to fulfill just that need. Our facility is open evenings, weekends and holidays because pets can get sick or injured at any time of day.


Located on the west side of Indianapolis, just minutes from downtown, our hospital provides emergency veterinary care to pet owners throughout central Indiana. Our expert staff of veterinarians and technicians are specially trained to handle emergency and critical care cases.

Meet Our Team

Dr. Jonathan Sheff
Dr. Sheff received his doctorate from Purdue University School of Veterinary Medicine in 1987. After a few years in general practice, he started working at the Airport Animal Emergi-Center in 1991. He was appointed Hospital Director and placed on the Board of Directors in 2006 and has held the position of President of the Board of Directors since 2010. He also practices primary care medicine and surgery once a week at Meridian Veterinary Hospital.

Dr. Brent Gust
Dr. Gust received his doctorate from Purdue University School of Veterinary Medicine in 2003. He as been associated with the Airport Animal Emergi-Center since 2005.

Dr. Robert Naum
Dr. Naum has been associated with Airport Animal Emergi-Center since its founding in 1982. He did his undergraduate training at Cornell, earning a BS in a wildlife and biology major. He graduated 2nd in Cornell’s veterinary class of 1978. He was selected for a rotating medical/surgical internship at the University of California Davis where he received the Best Intern of the Year award. After two years of general practice, he devoted the next 30 years to the emergency and critical care field. Currently, Dr Naum is on the Board of Directors for the clinic and continues to consult with the clinic’s full time veterinarians.

Dr. Alexander Murphy
Dr. Murphy received his doctorate from Oklahoma State University in 2013. After three years in general practice he transitioned to emergency medicine. His wife is also a practicing veterinarian here in Indianapolis.

Dr. Michelle Gray
Dr. Gray received her doctorate from Purdue University School of Veterinary Medicine in 2011. After growing up in Brownsburg, Dr. Gray attended Purdue University for both her undergraduate and doctorate education. Following graduation, she spend 1 year in Clinton, IN practicing companion animal medicine with a passion for Camelids (alpacas and llamas). Her husband’s education brought them back to the Indianapolis area where they continue to call home. Dr. Gray practiced as a general practitioner for a total of 5 1/2 years, doing relief work for Airport Animal Emergi-Center (AAEC) a couple times a month. In January of 2017, Dr. Gray joined AAEC full-time. In her free time, Dr. Gray enjoys traveling, yoga, indoor rock climbing, hiking and spending time with friends and family. She and her husband live in Carmel with their 1 cat, Nelly who came to them as a stray kitten.

Emergency & Critical Care Services

The Airport Animal Emergi-Center (AAEC) offers emergency care to dogs and cats when their veterinarian is unavailable. Our hospital is equipped with the resources necessary to help quickly and accurately diagnose and treat your injured or critically ill pet. Our highly skilled staff at the AAEC practice emergency and critical care nursing specifically for dogs and cats. Hospitalized patients are closely monitored, and our veterinarians will keep you informed of your pet's progress.

We can offer the following services to our patients:

On-Site Diagnostics

  • Blood Analysis
  • Blood Pressure Monitoring
  • Digital Radiography
  • Electrocardiography
  • Fecal Analysis
  • Infectious Disease Testing
  • Ocular Tonometry
  • Physical Exam by a Veterinarian
  • Ultrasound
  • Urinalysis

Treatments

  • Blood and Plasma Transfusions
  • In House Pharmacy
  • Intravenous Fluid and Medication Therapy
  • Oxygen Therapy
  • Pain Management
  • Toxin Ingestions
  • Venomous Snake Bites

Surgery

  • Emergency Surgery
  • Exploratory Surgery
  • Stabilization of Bone Fractures
